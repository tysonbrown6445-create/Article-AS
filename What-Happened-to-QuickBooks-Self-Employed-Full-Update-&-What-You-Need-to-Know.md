Many small business owners, freelancers, and independent contractors have asked: “What happened to QuickBooks Self-Employed?” Over the years, QuickBooks Self-Employed has evolved as Intuit refines its offerings to better serve the needs of self-employed professionals.

In this article, we’ll explain the changes, how the product works today, and what you should do if you’re impacted by access or login issues.

Overview: QuickBooks Self-Employed and Its Purpose
--------------------------------------------------

QuickBooks Self-Employed was designed for individuals who manage their own taxes, invoices, and expenses, especially gig workers and freelancers. It provides:

*   Simple expense tracking
    
*   Mileage tracking
    
*   Estimated quarterly tax calculations
    
*   Easy invoicing for clients
    

Users typically access their accounts via [QuickBooks Self-Employed Online Login](https://www.wizxpert.com/quickbooks-self-employed-login/) from any device.

Has QuickBooks Self-Employed Been Discontinued or Changed?
----------------------------------------------------------

QuickBooks Self-Employed is _not discontinued_, but Intuit has shifted how it fits into the overall QuickBooks ecosystem. In many regions, QuickBooks Self-Employed continues to be available as a standalone product, while in others it’s being positioned as an entry point before upgrading to QuickBooks Online.

This transition helps users eventually gain access to more advanced accounting tools without losing historical data.

Why Some Users Are Being Redirected or Transitioned
---------------------------------------------------

Some users have reported being redirected to the [QuickBooks Online login page](https://www.wizxpert.com/quickbooks-online-login-problems/) when signing in. This happens because:

*   Intuit is consolidating products to streamline features
    
*   Some Self-Employed accounts are being migrated to QuickBooks Online for enhanced reporting
    
*   Older versions of the Self-Employed app are being phased out in favor of unified platforms
    

If you experience issues signing in—or receive prompts to switch to QuickBooks Online, contact QuickBooks support at **1-844-405-0904** for help with your specific account.

What Features Are Still Available in QuickBooks Self-Employed
-------------------------------------------------------------

Even with changes, the core capabilities remain valuable for solo business owners:

*   Income and expense tracking
    
*   Simple invoicing
    
*   Mileage capture
    
*   Tax estimation and filing preparation support
    

If your business needs expand beyond these features, Intuit often suggests upgrading to QuickBooks Online, which supports deeper accounting capabilities and multi-user access.

Should You Stay on QuickBooks Self-Employed or Upgrade?

It depends on your needs:

### Stay on QuickBooks Self-Employed if:
