Step 1: Keep Income and Expenses Updated
----------------------------------------

Before filing taxes, make sure all transactions are accurate and categorized correctly.

*   Review imported bank transactions
    
*   Add missing income manually
    
*   Categorize expenses properly for deductions
    

Accurate data ensures correct tax calculations and avoids filing errors.

Step 2: Review Mileage and Deductions
-------------------------------------

QuickBooks Self-Employed automatically tracks mileage and deductible expenses. Review these carefully to maximize deductions and reduce your taxable income.

If you’re unsure about deductible categories, working with a [QuickBooks ProAdvisor](https://www.wizxpert.com/find-a-quickbooks-proadvisor/) can help ensure compliance and accuracy.

Step 3: Check Quarterly Estimated Taxes
---------------------------------------

QuickBooks Self-Employed calculates estimated quarterly taxes based on your income and expenses. Reviewing these estimates regularly helps you avoid penalties and surprises at tax time.

Step 4: Export Tax Reports
--------------------------

When you’re ready to file, QuickBooks Self-Employed generates key tax reports, including:

*   Schedule C summary
    
*   Income and expense reports
    
*   Mileage deduction reports
    

These reports can be shared with your tax preparer or used for self-filing.
