How to Add Income in QuickBooks Self-Employed?
----------------------------------------------

Tracking income accurately is essential for freelancers, independent contractors, and sole proprietors. If you’re wondering how to add income in QuickBooks Self-Employed, the process is simple and flexible, whether your income is recorded automatically or entered manually. This guide walks you through the correct methods to ensure your financial records remain accurate and tax-ready.

Understanding Income Tracking in QuickBooks Self-Employed
---------------------------------------------------------

QuickBooks Self-Employed automatically pulls income from linked bank accounts, but you can also add income manually. Proper income tracking helps calculate quarterly taxes and gives you a clear picture of your profitability.

Method 1: Add Income Automatically via Bank Connection
------------------------------------------------------

### Step 1: Connect Your Bank Account

Link your business bank account to QuickBooks Self-Employed so income transactions import automatically.

### Step 2: Review and Categorize Income

When transactions appear, review them and categorize them as income. This ensures they’re included in your reports and tax calculations.

Method 2: Add Income Manually
-----------------------------

### Step 1: Create a New Transaction

If you receive cash, checks, or payments outside your bank feed, manually add the income in QuickBooks Self-Employed.

### Step 2: Enter Income Details

Add the date, amount, description, and category. Save the transaction to update your records.

If you’re unsure how to categorize income correctly, a [QuickBooks ProAdvisor](https://www.wizxpert.com/find-a-quickbooks-proadvisor/) can help ensure accuracy.

Avoid Common Income-Tracking Mistakes
-------------------------------------

*   Forgetting to categorize imported transactions
    
*   Duplicating income entries
    
*   Mixing personal and business income
    
*   Ignoring uncategorized transactions
    

Avoiding these mistakes keeps your financial data clean and reliable.
